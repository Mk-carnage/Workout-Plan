Blood Sugar Control

Metabolic Health · Insulin Sensitivity · Glucose Regulation

🩸 Add Anytime — 2–3× Per Week

🩸 Why This Matters

Exercise is one of the most powerful tools to lower blood sugar — muscles absorb glucose directly without insulin during activity.
Best time: 30–60 min after a meal — catches the blood sugar spike before it peaks.
Even a 10-minute walk after eating reduces post-meal blood sugar significantly.
Mix: Resistance training improves long-term insulin sensitivity. Cardio burns glucose immediately.

🩸 Blood Sugar — Eat Right

✅ Eat This

🥗 Non-starchy vegetables (spinach, broccoli, beans)
🥚 Protein at every meal (eggs, dal, chicken)
🥜 Nuts + seeds — healthy fats slow glucose rise
🍋 Apple cider vinegar (1 tbsp in water before meals)
🌾 Brown rice or chapati over white rice
💧 3L+ water daily — hydration improves insulin response

❌ Avoid This

🍬 Sugary drinks, juices, maida, biscuits
🍚 Eating large rice portions alone — pair with protein
🍺 Alcohol — massive blood sugar disruption
😴 Sitting immediately after eating — take a walk
⏰ Skipping meals — causes blood sugar crashes
🛌 Eating heavy meals late at night

🩸 Blood Sugar Control Tips

Walk 10–15 min after every meal — most effective single habit to reduce post-meal blood sugar spikes.
Leg day is your best medicine. Large leg muscles are the body's biggest glucose sink — squats and lunges are blood sugar killers.
Eat protein first during meals, then vegetables, then carbs — this order reduces glucose spike by up to 30%.
Sleep is non-negotiable. Even one night of poor sleep raises blood sugar the next morning due to cortisol.
Never exercise on an empty stomach if you have low blood sugar episodes — eat a small snack 20 min before.
Consistency beats intensity. 30 min of moderate exercise daily beats one brutal session per week for blood sugar control.
⚠️ Important Note
If you are diabetic or pre-diabetic, consult your doctor before starting this protocol. Monitor your blood sugar before and after workouts. Carry a small snack during exercise in case of low blood sugar. This plan is designed for general fitness — not a replacement for medical treatment.
🔵 Ceiling Pulley Setup Guide
🪑

Position

Sit on a chair or kneel directly below the pulley. Keep back straight. Grip the rope or bar at shoulder width or wider.

🎯

Movement

Pull down to upper chest. Elbows drive down and back. Squeeze lats at bottom. Let weight rise slowly — 3 sec return.

⚖️

Load

Use a weight you can control for full range of motion. Increase load weekly. Avoid swinging the body to cheat reps.

🔁

Variations

Wide grip = outer lats. Close grip = inner lats + biceps. Underhand grip = more bicep. Face pull = rear delts.

📈 Progression Protocol
🔢

Add Reps

Add 1–2 reps each week. Week 1: 10 → Week 2: 12

➕

Add a Set

At top rep range, add a full set. 3 → 4 sets

🐢

Slow Tempo

3 sec lowering. More time under tension = more growth

⏩

Reduce Rest

Cut rest 10–15s weekly. Builds conditioning + fat loss
